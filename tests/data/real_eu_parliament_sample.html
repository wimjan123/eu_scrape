3. Debate on cases of breaches of human rights

García Pérez (PPE). – Madam President, I rise to speak about the urgent need to address human rights violations in several regions worldwide.

The situation in Myanmar remains deeply concerning. Since the military coup in 2021, we have witnessed systematic violations of fundamental rights. The international community must take decisive action.

Müller, Hans (S&D). – Thank you, Madam President. I would like to associate myself with the previous speaker's remarks.

However, I believe we must also focus on the humanitarian crisis in Afghanistan. The Taliban's restrictions on women's rights represent a clear violation of international law.

President. – I would like to remind speakers that we have limited time for this debate.

Rossi, Maria (GUE/NGL). – Madam President, while we debate human rights abroad, we must also address issues within our own borders.

The treatment of migrants at European borders raises serious concerns about our commitment to human dignity. We cannot have double standards.

Johnson, David (Renew). – Thank you, Madam President. I appreciate the contributions from my colleagues.

The European Union has always been a champion of human rights, both within and beyond our borders. We must continue this tradition through concrete actions, not just words.

President. – The debate is closed. We will now proceed to the vote.
